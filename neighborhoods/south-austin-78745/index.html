A buyer's guide from Crystal Kilpatrick

About the Area

Established South Austin, close to the core.

78745 along the South Lamar corridor is an older South Austin area with a mix of 1960s–1980s ranch houses, later infill, and low-rise multifamily along the arterials. Lots are generally smaller than in far-suburban tracts but larger than many core infill products, with tree cover and alley or driveway access depending on the block.

Commercial uses line South Lamar and Ben White, while interior streets remain primarily residential. The result is a ZIP that feels close to central Austin without the price ceiling of the inner-ring codes.

What You'll Love

Why buyers look here.

  • Direct access to South Lamar Boulevard with its continuous run toward downtown and Zilker Park.
  • Established single-family streets set back from South Lamar but still within a short drive of central Austin.
  • Proximity to US 290/71 (Ben White) and MoPac, giving multiple options to move east–west or north–south across the metro.

What to Know

Trade-offs worth checking before you offer.

  • South Lamar, Ben White, and MoPac can all experience notable congestion at peak commute hours, so off-peak drive times can be dramatically different from rush hour.
  • 78745 housing stock varies widely in age and level of renovation — on one block you may see original-condition mid-century homes next to recent remodels or small-lot new construction.
  • Some streets sit close to commercial and multifamily clusters along South Lamar, while others feel more insulated. Walk and drive the specific blocks you're considering at different times of day.

Schools

Austin ISD assignments.

Most of 78745 lies in Austin ISD. Depending on the exact address, common elementary feeders include Boone, Cowan, and Sunset Valley, with middle school feeders such as Covington or Bailey and high school feeders including Bowie or Crockett.

Because boundaries can shift, confirm the current AISD feeder pattern for any specific 78745 property using the district's interactive boundary map before writing an offer.

Commute & Access

The arterials matter more than the freeways here.

From South Lamar in 78745, off-peak drives to downtown via South Lamar or MoPac often fall in the 10–20 minute range. Ben White / US 290 ties the area directly into I-35 for north–south travel and continues east toward Austin-Bergstrom International Airport.

MoPac / Loop 1, South Lamar, and Manchaca Road are the primary north–south spines, and signal timing on these arterials strongly influences commute times. Test your specific route during your real commute window before you commit.

Local Lifestyle

What's nearby.

  • South Lamar corridor. A continuous commercial spine from Ben White north toward downtown, with restaurants, retail, and services lining the roadway.
  • Zilker Park area. Reachable by heading north on South Lamar, this large central green space and its surrounding amenities are a common destination for 78745 residents.
  • Ben White / US 290 retail clusters. Big-box retail and grocery options concentrate along Ben White just east of MoPac, providing daily-needs shopping within a short drive.
  • Westgate-area shopping. Grocery and specialty retail just northwest of much of 78745.
  • Southwest Austin trail and park network. A patchwork of city parks and nearby trail segments accessible via short drives from interior 78745 streets.

Crystal's Take

What I see on the ground in 78745.

In 78745 along the South Lamar corridor, my recent listings have closed at $1.39M, $1.31M, and $850K. Those transactions sat at the higher end of the neighborhood price band, reflecting larger or more updated properties in locations that connected well to central Austin.

When I work in this ZIP, I pay close attention to which side of South Lamar or Ben White a home falls on, how it sits relative to MoPac, and what's happening with nearby infill and commercial redevelopment. Interior blocks can feel very different from the arterial frontage, so a quick drive-through rarely tells the whole story.

For buyers who want to stay inside the more central ring without stepping all the way into the core ZIP codes, 78745 remains one I watch closely.

FAQ

Common questions about 78745.

How competitive is the South Austin 78745 market right now?

Recent Redfin data show 78745 as somewhat competitive, with homes receiving about one offer on average and a recent median sale price in the low- to mid-$400Ks. Days on market have been in the roughly 80-day range — slower than peak-cycle years but still active for central-adjacent inventory.

What types of homes are common in 78745?

Most of the single-family housing stock dates from the 1960s through the 1980s, with one-story ranches and modest two-story homes on interior streets. Infill projects and townhome-style units are more common along or near South Lamar and Ben White, where zoning supports more intense use.

How far is 78745 from downtown Austin?

From many 78745 addresses along South Lamar, off-peak drives to downtown are around 10–20 minutes by car. Commute times stretch during rush hour, especially at MoPac and Ben White interchanges, so I advise clients to test their route at realistic times.

Which school district covers 78745?

78745 is in Austin ISD, with several elementary campuses (including Boone, Cowan, and Sunset Valley) and middle/high school feeders such as Covington, Bailey, Bowie, and Crockett depending on the street. Because boundaries evolve, I always confirm a specific address against the AISD boundary map before a buyer writes an offer.

What are typical lot sizes in South Austin 78745?

Lots in 78745 are typically suburban-scale city lots, often around one-eighth to one-quarter of an acre, though this varies by subdivision. Compared to newer fringe subdivisions, you'll often see more mature trees and less uniform lot dimensions.

How does 78745 pricing compare to the broader Austin market?

In late 2025, 78745's median sale price in the low-$400Ks–$470Ks sat below the citywide Austin median, which has hovered around the low-$500Ks over similar periods. Within the ZIP, though, renovated homes near South Lamar and MoPac can still trade well above that median.
