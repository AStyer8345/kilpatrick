About the Area

Plum Creek east. Master-planned west. Old Town in the middle.

Kyle sits about 20 miles south of downtown Austin in Hays County, between Buda to the north and San Marcos to the south along I-35. The 78640 ZIP covers the City of Kyle plus stretches of unincorporated Hays County. Two anchors distinguish 78640 from the rest of the I-35 south corridor: Plum Creek, a New Urbanism master-planned community east of I-35 with mixed-use density, front-porch lot patterns, and a town-center retail node — and Old Town Kyle, the Center Street historic district that pre-dates the modern I-35 corridor by more than a century. Newer growth concentrates west of I-35 in subdivisions like Hometown Kyle, Six Creeks, Kyle Crossing, and Bunton Creek.

Detached single-family is the dominant housing type, but the product mix in 78640 is wider than in most south-corridor markets. Plum Creek east of I-35 combines traditional detached homes with townhomes, garage apartments, and small-lot New Urbanism layouts. West of I-35, the inventory is largely standard master-planned subdivisions from the 2000s through 2020s. The Old Town Kyle core east of I-35 contains a smaller cluster of older homes from the late 1800s through mid-century.

What You'll Love

Why buyers look here.

  • Plum Creek's New Urbanism layout east of I-35 — front-porch street patterns, integrated townhomes and detached homes, and a town-center retail node that you won't find in a standard I-35 south master-plan.
  • Old Town Kyle's Center Street historic district, with restaurants, City Square Park, and railroad-era buildings inside a compact walkable downtown corridor.
  • Seton Medical Center Hays on the west side of I-35 along Kyle Parkway — a regional hospital campus with adjacent medical-office plaza, which keeps a meaningful share of healthcare jobs and services inside the city. ---

What to Know

Trade-offs worth checking before you offer.

  • The downtown Austin commute from Kyle runs longer than from Buda — generally 30–40 minutes off-peak versus 25–35 from Buda — and peak periods on I-35 north of SH 45 can extend the inbound trip materially. Drive your route at your real commute hours before committing.
  • East-of-I-35 and west-of-I-35 are different products inside the same ZIP. East of I-35 means Plum Creek's New Urbanism mix or older Old Town homes. West of I-35 means standard 2000s–2020s master-planned subdivisions. The two sides aren't interchangeable on lifestyle, age of inventory, or HOA structure.
  • Many master-planned subdivisions in 78640 sit inside Municipal Utility Districts (MUDs) with assessments on top of city and county property taxes. Total effective tax rate can vary by hundreds of dollars per month between a MUD-overlay parcel and a parcel inside city limits without a MUD — verify the full rate by parcel. ---

Schools

Hays CISD assignments — Lehman High School at the center.

78640 falls within Hays CISD. Elementary schools serving the area include Hemphill, Negley, Tobias, and Kyle Elementary among others, with assignment varying by address. Middle school feeders include Wallace Middle, Armando Chapa Middle, and others depending on subdivision. At the high-school level, Lehman High School in Kyle is the primary feeder for most 78640 addresses, with some northern sections feeding instead to Hays High or Johnson High in the Buda area.

Hays CISD attendance zones have been adjusted multiple times in recent years to keep pace with district growth. Verify the current elementary, middle, and high school assignment for any specific Kyle address using the Hays CISD attendance-zone tools before relying on it for an offer.

Commute & Access

I-35 north for Austin, south for San Marcos and Texas State.

I-35 is the primary corridor in and out of 78640, with main local exits at FM 150 / Center Street, Kyle Parkway, and Yarrington Road. Off-peak, downtown Austin is generally 30–35 minutes north via I-35; peak periods extend that, particularly through the SH 45 / Slaughter Lane corridor. Texas State University and downtown San Marcos are roughly 10–15 minutes south on I-35.

Austin-Bergstrom International Airport is typically 25–35 minutes via SH 45 toll to SH 71. SH 21 connects east-west to Lockhart and the eastern metro, useful for reverse commutes that avoid central I-35.

Local Lifestyle

What's nearby.

  • Plum Creek Town Center. A mixed-use retail and dining node within the Plum Creek master-planned community east of I-35, with restaurants, services, and a community pavilion.
  • Old Town Kyle / Center Street. The historic downtown district east of I-35, with restaurants, the City Square Park, and railroad-era buildings dating to the late 1800s.
  • Downtown Austin. Roughly 30–40 minutes north via I-35, with the central business district, Capitol complex, and Lady Bird Lake hike-and-bike trail.
  • Driftwood & Wimberley. West via FM 150 to Salt Lick BBQ in Driftwood, Driftwood wineries, and the Wimberley square — generally 25–40 minutes.
  • San Marcos Premium Outlets & Tanger. Roughly 10–15 minutes south on I-35 in San Marcos, with two adjacent outlet malls covering most national brands. ---

Crystal's Take

What I see on the ground in 78640.

I've closed two homes in 78640 in the last 24 months — a $350K sale and a $324K sale. That's the entry-level / first-move-up Hays County pattern, and it's where Kyle still earns its place on the I-35 south corridor: a buyer who's been priced out of Buda or south Austin can typically buy more square footage and a newer home in 78640, even after factoring in the longer commute.

When I'm working with buyers in Kyle, the first conversation is east-of-I-35 versus west-of-I-35. East means Plum Creek's New Urbanism layout or an older home in Old Town — different lifestyles than what you'll find anywhere else on the corridor. West means standard 2000s–2020s master-planned subdivisions. The next conversation is the Lehman High School question, since 78640 mostly feeds Lehman rather than the Hays / Johnson High split that defines Buda. The third is taxes — MUD overlays in some master-planned subdivisions push the total rate noticeably higher than non-MUD parcels of similar value.

For first-time buyers and move-up buyers who need Austin job access at a price south Austin no longer supports, 78640 is one of the most realistic plays on the corridor. For sellers, the same I-35 access that brings buyers in also means competing inventory in Buda and San Marcos is one exit away — pricing discipline matters here.

FAQ

Common questions about 78640.

Is Kyle the same as Buda?
No. Kyle and Buda are separate cities in Hays County along the I-35 south corridor. Buda sits about 15 miles south of downtown Austin; Kyle is another five miles farther south, between Buda and San Marcos. The two share the Hays CISD school district but feed different high schools — Lehman primarily for Kyle, Hays or Johnson for Buda.

What school district serves Kyle?
Hays CISD covers 78640. Most addresses feed to Lehman High School in Kyle, with some northern sections feeding to Hays High or Johnson High in the Buda area. Elementary and middle assignments vary by subdivision — verify the current zone for a specific address using Hays CISD's tools.

What's the median home price in 78640?
Recent Redfin data put the 78640 median in the low-to-mid $300Ks range across 2025, with substantial variation by subdivision and home age. Newer master-planned subdivisions west of I-35 trend higher than entry-level inventory; Plum Creek and Old Town are their own micromarkets within the ZIP.

How long is the commute from Kyle to downtown Austin?
Generally 30–40 minutes via I-35 in typical conditions, with peak periods running longer depending on conditions north of SH 45. SH 45 toll provides an east-west connection useful for reverse commutes or trips that avoid central I-35.

What's Plum Creek?
Plum Creek is a New Urbanism master-planned community on the east side of I-35 in Kyle, with a mix of detached homes, townhomes, garage apartments, and a town-center retail node. The street pattern emphasizes front-porch interaction, sidewalks, and pedestrian connectivity, which is unusual for a south-corridor master-plan and makes it a different lifestyle product than the standard subdivisions west of I-35.

Are there homes in Old Town Kyle worth considering?
Yes — the Old Town Kyle core east of I-35 along Center Street contains a smaller cluster of older homes from the late 1800s through mid-century. These are a different product than the master-planned inventory, with different lot sizes, deed restrictions, utility setups, and renovation considerations. Worth touring on their own terms rather than comparing directly to a 2015 west-side subdivision home.
