The Process

How buying with Crystal works.

A clean path from first call to keys.

1. Schedule a strategy call

Map timeline, budget, neighborhoods, financing, school zones, and deal-breakers. Leave with a working brief and next step.

2. Get pre-approved

Get a current pre-approval from a real lender before touring seriously or writing an offer.

3. Build the brief

Turn the wish list into a usable filter: ZIPs, schools, commute, tax tolerance, must-haves, and walk-away issues.

4. Tour homes — including pre-MLS and off-market

Review public MLS inventory plus private-network opportunities through Austin Luxury Network, Broker Underground, and Dripping Springs Elite.

5. Make a strategic offer

Structure price, financing, option period, closing date, and concessions so the offer is competitive without being careless.

6. Inspections and option period

Use inspections to negotiate repairs, credits, or a price adjustment — or to walk away clean when the house is wrong.

7. Under contract to closing

Amy Goldberg tracks appraisal, survey, title, HOA docs, lender deadlines, walk-through, and closing logistics.

8. After-close concierge

After closing, Crystal stays available for vendors, remodelers, cleaners, painters, and future market questions.

How does buyer-agent compensation work after the NAR settlement?

Since August 2024, Texas buyers must sign a written buyer-representation agreement before touring homes with an agent. The agreement spells out the agent's compensation — paid by the buyer, the seller, the listing brokerage, or some combination depending on what's negotiated on each transaction.

Crystal walks every buyer through the agreement on the front end so the cost structure is clear before any home is toured. There are no surprise fees at closing. This is a summary, not legal advice — your attorney can answer specific contract questions.
