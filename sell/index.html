Our Home-Selling Process

Nine steps from listing agreement to closing day.

  1. 01

    Hire a REALTOR®

    Market research, pricing strategy, and a tailored marketing plan. The Listing Agreement is signed and the work begins — staging timeline, photo schedule, and launch date all locked in.

  2. 02

    Home Preparation & Staging Consultation

    Declutter, light touch-ups, or full staging — whatever the home needs to show at its best for photos, in-person showings, and online marketing. The decision is informed by the price point and what the comparable inventory looks like.

  3. 03

    Strategic Marketing Launch

    Pre-MLS Coming Soon distribution through Austin Luxury Network, Broker Underground, and the monthly Dripping Springs Elite property tour. Professional photography goes out, social and digital campaigns go live, and the listing syndicates to every major search portal. The home gets visibility before it ever hits the public MLS.

  4. 04

    Showings & Open Houses

    A showing schedule that fits the seller's life while maximizing buyer exposure. Private showings on request, plus expertly marketed open houses with feedback tracked and reported back.

  5. 05

    Receiving & Negotiating Offers

    Every offer gets evaluated on terms, not just price — contingencies, financing strength, and closing timelines included. The highest offer is not always the best offer. CNE and RENE training shows up here.

  6. 06

    Under Contract

    Outline the next steps and the full timeline. Walk the seller through every detail of the contract process so nothing gets missed and nothing gets lost between the option period and the funding date.

  7. 07

    Inspections & Repairs

    Most buyers conduct an inspection. Negotiate repairs and credits where it makes sense to keep the deal moving while protecting the seller's bottom line. Crystal's vendor network handles the work that has to happen.

  8. 08

    Packing & Moving Coordination

    Moving resources, utility transfer reminders, and last-minute checklists so closing week is not a fire drill. Amy Goldberg keeps the document trail and the deadline calendar.

  9. 09

    Closing Day

    Schedule and attend closing at the title company. The job is not done until the keys are handed over.

Staging

The simplest way to add value before launch.

Staging is a simple way to receive maximum value on a listing. The photos look better. The home shows better. Buyers walking through (in person or on a phone) can picture themselves in the rooms instead of trying to mentally erase the seller's furniture.

Crystal's listings get a staging consultation as part of the listing agreement. Sometimes that means a full furniture stage. Sometimes it means moving four pieces and removing six. Either way, the decision gets made before the photographer shows up — not after.

Professional Photography

Photos sell the showing.

Photos are one of the most important elements in a listing. A professional real estate photographer captures the home's best selling points and walks a prospective buyer through the property visually — telling a story room by room. In most cases the photos are what determine whether a buyer requests a showing or scrolls past. Done badly, they are also what kills a listing before anyone walks through the front door. Homes sell faster and for more money with top-quality photography. Every Crystal Kilpatrick listing gets it.

Home Value

Get a real number — not a Zestimate.

An algorithm cannot walk through a home. It does not know the kitchen was remodeled in 2024 or that the back fence was replaced last spring. It does not see how the lot sits or how the floor plan flows. A pricing conversation with Crystal pulls active inventory, sales inside 30 days, days on market, and the rate environment into a defensible number for the actual property — not for the average property in the ZIP.

The fastest way to start: a 15-minute call. Crystal will pull the comparables before the call so the conversation is about the number, not the data gathering.

FAQ

Important questions to ask any listing agent.

Are you a full-time or part-time agent?

Full-time. Real estate has been Crystal's only profession since 2000. The Crystal Kilpatrick Group is a full-time team — Crystal as listing agent, Kelly Gosey on buyer and seller representation, and Amy Goldberg on transaction coordination. A side-job agent and a full-time team handle a listing very differently when something breaks at the eleventh hour.

Do you have a dedicated team?

Yes. Selling a home involves marketing, scheduling, paperwork, negotiation, and a calendar of contractual deadlines. Amy Goldberg runs transaction coordination — contracts, vendor coordination, and the document trail behind every closing. Kelly Gosey represents clients alongside Crystal and brings a home-design and remodeling background that pays off when sellers are weighing pre-listing improvements.

What's your plan of action for marketing a listing?

Multi-channel and named, not generic. Professional photography and videography, full-service staging, pre-MLS Coming Soon distribution through Austin Luxury Network, Broker Underground, and the monthly Dripping Springs Elite property tour, exposure across five Central Texas MLS databases, syndication to every major real estate site, strategic social and digital marketing, and direct outreach to Crystal's agent network for qualified buyers.

How well do you know the market?

26 years in Austin. 1,000+ career closings. 22 listings closed in the trailing 24 months alone, ranging from a $233,000 starter home in San Marcos to a $3.15 million Barton Creek estate. Crystal pulls active inventory, recent sales inside 30 days, average days on market, and rate-environment context for every listing conversation. The pricing decision is data-anchored, not a guess.
